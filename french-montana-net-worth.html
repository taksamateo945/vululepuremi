He was born in Casablanca, Morocco, and moved to the United States with his family when he was 13 years old.

French Montana started his musical career in 2002, releasing independent mixtapes and collaborating with other artists.

He signed a joint-venture deal with Bad Boy Records and Maybach Music Group in 2012, and released his debut studio album Excuse My French in 2013.

Some of his hit songs include “Pop That”, “Unforgettable”, and “Mopstick”. 

French Montana is also the founder of Coke Boys Records, a label that has signed several talented artists.

French Montana is not only a successful rapper, but also a philanthropist who has supported various causes and charities.

He is the most streamed African-born musical artist, and the first African-born artist to have a diamond-certified song.
